# Sales & Discounts Analysis Project

## Descriptive Analytics and Data Preprocessing on Sales & Discounts Dataset

## Project Overview

This project focuses on performing complete descriptive analytics and data preprocessing on a Sales & Discounts dataset to understand pricing behavior, discount patterns, sales distribution, and revenue concentration.

The project demonstrates how statistical analysis and visualization can help businesses identify high-value transactions, optimize discount strategies, and improve profitability decisions.

This is a strong real-world Data Analytics project involving descriptive statistics, outlier detection, visualization, and business insight generation.

---

## Problem Statement

The objective of this project is to analyze sales transactions and identify:

* pricing patterns
* discount behavior
* revenue concentration
* abnormal transactions
* profitability opportunities
* business decision-support insights

using statistical summaries and exploratory analysis techniques.

This project helps answer:

### Which transactions generate the highest revenue?

### Are discounts helping or hurting profitability?

### Where is revenue concentrated inside the business?

---

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains important business variables such as:

* **Volume** → Number of units sold
* **Avg Price** → Average selling price
* **Total Sales Value** → Gross transaction value
* **Discount Rate (%)** → Percentage discount offered
* **Discount Amount** → Total discount value
* **Net Sales Value** → Final sales value after discount

These variables help understand sales performance, pricing efficiency, and discount effectiveness.

---

### 3. Outlier Detection

Used IQR (Interquartile Range) method for outlier detection

IQR = Q3 - Q1

This helped identify:

* unusually high-value transactions
* extreme discount values
* abnormal pricing patterns

Outliers were carefully interpreted instead of blindly removed.

## Key Insights

### Most financial variables showed strong right-skewed distributions

This indicates that while most transactions were small to medium in size, a small number of high-value transactions generated a major portion of total revenue.

---

### Large differences between Mean and Median confirmed revenue concentration

This showed that revenue was heavily dependent on fewer premium transactions.

---

### Discount Rate (%) showed relatively controlled variation

This suggests discount policies were more standardized compared to pricing and transaction values.

---

### Outliers in Avg Price, Total Sales Value, and Net Sales Value were business-critical

These may represent premium customers, high-value products, or strategic enterprise sales and should be studied carefully instead of removed blindly.
